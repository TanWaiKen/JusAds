# Platform Creative Intelligence for Video Ad Directors

Use the appropriate platform section below based on the target platform. These are real patterns from high-performing ads on each platform — not theoretical guidelines.

---

## TikTok / Reels (9:16 Vertical)

### What Works
- **Native UGC style** outperforms polished ads 3-5x. Ads that look like content get watched.
- **Hook in 0-1.5s**: First frame must interrupt scrolling. Top patterns:
  - Bold text overlay: "POV: you finally found the perfect [X]"
  - Unexpected visual (product falling, macro shot, before/after)
  - Direct address: person looking into camera with an urgent opener
  - Sound-driven: trending audio that people recognize instantly
- **Trending sound cues**: Even without using copyrighted audio, reference the ENERGY of trending sounds. Fast beat = fast cuts. Emotional ballad = slow reveal. Bass drop = product reveal moment.
- **Scene pacing**: 2-3 seconds per scene max. If a scene can be split, split it.
- **Text overlays mandatory**: 70%+ watch without sound. Every key message must be on screen.
- **CTA style**: "Link in bio", "Comment [word] for link", "Save this for later". Never a formal CTA card.
- **Pattern interrupt at scene 2-3**: When attention wanes, change something — angle, color, speed.

### What Fails
- Corporate/polished look (instantly skipped)
- Slow intros without text (lost in 1 second)
- No subtitles/overlays
- Generic stock footage feeling
- Hard-sell language without social proof

### Ad Structure (15s)
| Scene | Time | Purpose | Style |
|-------|------|---------|-------|
| 1 | 0-3s | HOOK — stop the scroll | Bold text + arresting visual |
| 2 | 3-7s | PROBLEM/RELATE — "you know when..." | Personal, relatable shot |
| 3 | 7-12s | PRODUCT REVEAL — satisfying moment | Close-up, unboxing, demo |
| 4 | 12-15s | CTA — "get yours" | Product + text overlay |

### Ad Structure (30s)
| Scene | Time | Purpose | Style |
|-------|------|---------|-------|
| 1 | 0-3s | HOOK | Pattern interrupt |
| 2 | 3-8s | PROBLEM | Relatable scenario |
| 3 | 8-13s | SOLUTION INTRO | Product appears naturally |
| 4 | 13-20s | PROOF/DEMO | Product in use, results |
| 5 | 20-25s | SOCIAL PROOF | Reactions, reviews, numbers |
| 6 | 25-30s | CTA | Direct instruction + product |

### Sound Design Direction
- Background beat should feel like a trending TikTok sound (upbeat, rhythmic)
- Bass drop or beat switch at product reveal
- Voiceover style: casual, conversational, NOT announcer-voice
- Consider: lo-fi, chill beats, or energetic pop depending on product energy

## Instagram (Feed / Stories / Reels — 1:1 or 9:16)

### What Works
- **More polished than TikTok** but still authentic. Think "elevated lifestyle content."
- **Aesthetic-first**: Color grading, composition, and visual harmony matter more here.
- **Hook patterns**:
  - Beautiful product flat-lay or lifestyle shot
  - "New drop" / "Just launched" text over aspirational scene
  - Carousel-style reveals in video format (frame 1 teaser → frame 2 detail)
- **Brand consistency**: Instagram audiences expect visual brand identity — consistent color palette, fonts, styling.
- **Mid-length captions work**: People read captions on IG. Pair strong visual with CTA in voiceover.
- **Influencer/creator-style narration**: First-person "I've been using this for 2 weeks and..."

### What Fails
- Raw/unpolished UGC (feels out of place)
- Overly sales-y tone
- Ignoring brand aesthetic for shock value
- No visual cohesion between scenes

### Ad Structure (15s)
| Scene | Time | Purpose | Style |
|-------|------|---------|-------|
| 1 | 0-3s | BEAUTY SHOT — stop scroll with aesthetics | Cinematic product/lifestyle |
| 2 | 3-8s | STORY — create desire | Person using/enjoying product |
| 3 | 8-12s | DETAIL — zoom in | Product features, texture, craft |
| 4 | 12-15s | CTA — aspirational close | Brand moment + "Shop now" |

### Sound Design Direction
- Clean, ambient music bed (lo-fi, acoustic, or minimal electronic)
- Voiceover: warm, confident, slightly intimate
- Sound effects: subtle (package opening, fabric swoosh) — nothing jarring
- Music should feel like a curated playlist, not a trend chase

## YouTube (Pre-roll / Shorts — 16:9 or 9:16)

### What Works — Pre-roll (16:9)
- **5-second rule**: Viewers skip after 5 seconds. Your ENTIRE value proposition must land in the first 5 seconds.
- **Pattern**: Brand/product in frame from second 0. Don't build up.
- **Direct address works**: Person looking at camera, speaking fast with conviction.
- **Problem-solution compression**: "Tired of X? [Product] does Y in Z seconds."
- **Longer format available**: After the 5s hook, you have 15-25 more seconds to elaborate for those who stay.

### What Works — Shorts (9:16)
- Hybrid of TikTok energy + YouTube production quality
- How-to / tutorial format performs well
- "Watch till the end" hooks drive completion
- Educational + entertaining = best combo

### What Fails
- Slow brand reveals (skipped instantly)
- Cinematic opening without product context
- Treating it like TV (this isn't a 30-second TV spot)
- Logos without context in first 5 seconds

### Ad Structure (15s Pre-roll)
| Scene | Time | Purpose | Style |
|-------|------|---------|-------|
| 1 | 0-5s | FULL MESSAGE — brand + benefit + product | Direct, clear, fast |
| 2 | 5-10s | EXPAND — proof or demo | Product in action |
| 3 | 10-15s | CLOSE — CTA + brand | Clear next step |

### Sound Design Direction
- Voiceover-forward: must be crisp and clear from second 0
- Background music lower than TikTok — voice carries the message
- Professional narration style (not UGC casual, not corporate stiff)

## Shopee / E-commerce (1:1 or 9:16)

### What Works
- **Price/deal-forward**: Lead with the offer, discount, or value proposition.
- **Product showcase focus**: Close-ups, multi-angle, in-use demonstrations.
- **Urgency mechanics**: Countdown overlay, "limited stock", flash sale framing.
- **Trust builders**: Ratings, review snippets, "X sold" counters.
- **Before/after**: Especially for beauty, cleaning, food products.
- **Unboxing energy**: Show what arrives, unwrapping, first-use satisfaction.

### What Fails
- Lifestyle without product clarity
- No price/deal mentioned
- Abstract brand storytelling (this is a purchase platform)
- Slow reveals of what the product actually is

### Ad Structure (15s)
| Scene | Time | Purpose | Style |
|-------|------|---------|-------|
| 1 | 0-3s | DEAL/HOOK — price or offer | Big text + product |
| 2 | 3-8s | PRODUCT DEMO — what it does | Close-up, in-use |
| 3 | 8-12s | PROOF — reviews/ratings/results | Social proof overlay |
| 4 | 12-15s | URGENCY CTA — "Buy now" | Deal + platform button |

### Sound Design Direction
- Energetic, sale-event energy (think "flash sale" music)
- Voiceover: enthusiastic, deal-focused, fast-paced
- Sound effects: cash register, notification dings, "cha-ching"
- Keep it bright and transactional — this drives purchases, not brand love

## Creative Reference: What Makes Great Ads Memorable

Real-world patterns that the Director should consider when planning scenes:

### Sound-First Ads (like WangLaoji / Halland)
- The MUSIC drives the edit — cuts happen on beats
- Product rhythm: pour on the beat, reveal on the drop
- Jingle or catchy phrase becomes the hook (think "Red Bull gives you wings")
- For our system: Plan scenes that HIT BEATS even without the final music — the editor/assembler will align

### Emotional Arc Ads
- Setup a relatable struggle → product saves the day → emotional payoff
- Character's expression tells the story more than words
- Works best for: skincare, health, parenting, self-improvement

### Demonstration Ads
- Product IS the star — no character needed
- Macro shots, slow-motion, satisfying ASMR moments
- Works best for: food, cleaning, tech gadgets, beauty tools

### Social Proof Ads
- Multiple rapid testimonials/reactions
- Before/after split screens
- Numbers/stats overlaid on product shots
- Works best for: supplements, services, apps, courses
